Boy (Narrator):
Ek samay ki baat hai, ek shikari tha jo jungle mein praaniyon ko aadhe mein maar daalta tha. Jaanwaron ko bhayanak peeda mein chhod diya jaata tha, aur Narad Muni jo wahan se guzar rahe the, unhone ye dekha aur socha, "Kaun ye sab kar raha hai?"

Unhone dekha ki praaniyon ko aadha maara gaya tha aur vo peeda se karra rahe the. Unhone socha, "Is vyakti ko inhein poora maar daalne ke bajaaye, aadha kyun maar raha hai?"

[Scene 2: Narad Muni Confronts the Hunter]

(Narad Muni walks through the forest and encounters Mrigaari, the hunter, with his bow and arrows. Parvat Muni accompanies him.)

Narad Muni:
Tum ye jaanwaron ko aadha kyun maar rahe ho? Poora maar do inhein, taaki ye itni peeda mein na rahein.

Mrigaari (Hunter):
Mujhe nahi pata tha ki inhein maarna paap hai. Mujhe toh mere pita ne yahi sikhaya hai, aur yahi mera pesha hai. Main nahi jaanta tha ki ye paap hai.

Narad Muni:
Ye paap hai, aur tumhe iska bhog bhugatna padega. Tumne kabhi nahi suna ki is tarah se praaniyon ko maarna vishesh roop se paap hai?

Parvat Muni:
Mrigari, tum apne karmon ka asar nahi samajh pa rahe ho. Yeh praaniyon ki peeda sirf inka dukh nahi hai, balki tumhare bhavishya ka bojh banega. Karm ka fal tumhe kisi na kisi roop mein zaroor prapt hota hai.

Mrigaari (Hunter):
Yeh pehli baar hai jab main aapse sun raha hoon ki is tarah se maarna paap hai. Mujhe yeh nahi pata tha.

Narad Muni:
Agar tum is paapi jeevan se mukti paana chahte ho, toh main tumhe ek marg dikhata hoon.

[Scene 3: Narad Muni Guides the Hunter]

Narad Muni:
Main tumhe apna shishya banata hoon, aur tumhe ek mantra pradaan karta hoon. Tum Hare Krishna maha mantra ka jaap karo, aur Ganga ke tat par baith jao.

Mrigaari (Hunter):
Lekin muni, mera bhojan ka kya hoga? Main apne liye bhojan kahan se launga?

Parvat Muni:
Narad Muni ka aashirwad tumhare saath hai, Mrigaari. Chinta mat karo, Bhagwan tumhari madad karenge. Tumhe jo zaroorat hogi, sab mil jayega.

Narad Muni:
Haan, tumhe bhojan milta rahega. Tum iski chinta mat karo.

[Scene 4: The Hunter's Transformation]

(As the news spreads in the village, people start visiting the hunter, bringing food and supplies.)

STORY-2(

[Scene 1: Narad Muni in Vaikuntha]

(The scene opens with Narad Muni sitting in meditation in Vaikuntha, the abode of Lord Vishnu. A soft, divine light fills the stage, representing Lord Vishnu's presence.)

BOY1 (Narrator):
Yeh kahani hai Narad Muni, ek mochi aur ek Brahmin ki, jo apne apne jeevan mein Bhagwan Vishnu ki bhakti karte hain. Ek din, Narad Muni Vaikuntha mein apne prabhu, Bhagwan Vishnu ke samaksh baithe the.

Narad Muni:
Prabhu, main dharti par jaane se pehle, kya aapke bhakton ko aapka koi sandesh pradaan kar sakta hoon?

Lord Vishnu (voice):
Narad, tum mere param bhakton mein se ho. Tum dharti par jao aur apne bhakton ki bhakti ki pariksha lo. Tumhe ek Brahmin aur ek mochi milenge, jo apni bhakti mein meri stuti karte hain. Tum in dono se milo aur dekhna ki sahi bhakti kya hoti hai.

Narad Muni:
Aapka aadesh shirodharya prabhu. Main abhi dharti par jaata hoon.

(Narad Muni folds his hands, bows, and exits the stage as the scene transitions to Earth.)

[Scene 2: Narad Muni Meets the Brahmin]

(The scene changes to a Brahmin’s house. The Brahmin is performing his daily rituals. Narad Muni enters.)

BOY1 (Narrator):
Narad Muni dharti par aaye aur pehle us Brahmin se milne gaye, jo apni bhakti mein dooba hua tha.

Brahmin:
Pranam, Narad Muni. Main aapka swagat karta hoon. Kripya bataiye, aapke prabhu ka kya sandesh hai?

Narad Muni:
Pranam, Brahmin dev. Bhagwan Vishnu ne aapki bhakti ko dekha hai. Kya aapko apne jeevan ke baare mein koi prashna hai?

Brahmin:
Haan, Narad Muni. Kya aap mere prabhu se puchh sakte hain ki mujhe moksha kab prapt hoga?

Narad Muni:
Main aapka prashna apne prabhu ke samaksh rakhunga. Main jab wapas aaunga, to tumhe tumhare prashno ka uttar zaroor doonga.

(Narad Muni exits to continue his journey to meet the cobbler.)

[Scene 3: Narad Muni Meets the Cobbler]

(The scene changes to a small, humble village setting. The cobbler is sitting under a tree, mending shoes, and chanting the names of Lord Vishnu. Narad Muni enters.)

BOY1 (Narrator):
Narad Muni uske baad us mochi se milne chale gaye jise Bhagwan Vishnu ne unse milne ke liye kaha tha. Mochi apni dincharya mein vyast tha, lekin uski jeevan mein har pal bhagwan ka smaran tha.

Cobbler:
(humming softly)
Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya... Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya...

Narad Muni:
Pranam, bhakta. Tumhari bhakti sach mein prashansaniya hai. Main Bhagwan Vishnu ka sandesh lekar aaya hoon. Tumhe kisi bhi prakaar ka prashna ho, tum mujhse puchh sakte ho.

Cobbler:
Pranam Narad Muni. Main apne prabhu se sirf ek hi sawal puchhna chahta hoon. Mujhe ye jaanna hai ki mere jeevan ka anth kab hoga? Aur kya main apni bhakti ke kshetra mein safal ho paunga?

Narad Muni:
Main tumhara prashna apne prabhu ke samaksh rakhunga. Main jab wapas aaunga, to tumhe tumhare prashno ka uttar zaroor doonga.

(Narad Muni nods, then exits the scene as the cobbler returns to his work, continuing to chant.)

[Scene 4: Narad Muni's Return to Vaikuntha]

(The scene returns to Vaikuntha, where Narad Muni stands before Lord Vishnu.)

Narad Muni:
Prabhu, main aapke bhakt Brahmin aur mochi se mila. Dono ne mujhse kuch prashna kiye hain. Brahmin ne moksha ke baare mein puchha hai, aur mochi apne jeevan aur bhakti ke baare mein jaana chahta hai.

Lord Vishnu (voice):
Narad, tum Brahmin se kehna ki uska moksha abhi door hai. Aur mochi ko kehna ki uska anth samay aa raha hai, lekin uske jeevan mein jo bhi kuch hota hai, vah sab meri ichcha se hota hai. Tum in dono ko ye bhi kehna ki main is samay ek haathi ko soyi ke chhed se nikaal raha hoon.

Narad Muni:
(a bit puzzled but trusting the Lord)
Main aapka sandesh un tak zaroor pahunchaunga, Prabhu.

(Narad Muni bows and exits the scene, heading back to Earth.)

[Scene 5: Narad Muni Returns to the Brahmin]

(The scene shifts back to the Brahmin's house. Narad Muni returns to the Brahmin, who is eagerly awaiting his answer.)

Brahmin:
Pranam, Narad Muni. Kya aap mere prashna ka uttar lekar aaye hain?

Narad Muni:
Haan, Brahmin dev. Bhagwan Vishnu ne kaha hai ki tumhara moksha abhi door hai. Aur, unhone ye bhi kaha hai ki is samay vah ek haathi ko soyi ke chhed se nikaal rahe hain.

Brahmin:
(laughs mockingly)
Narad Muni, yeh kaise sambhav ho sakta hai? Haathi itna bada hota hai aur soyi ka chhed itna chhota! Aap kaise is baat par vishwas kar sakte hain?

Narad Muni:
(with a hint of doubt)
Mujhe apne prabhu par poora vishwas hai. Lekin main is uttar ko lekar kuch chintit hoon.

(Narad Muni leaves the Brahmin and proceeds to meet the cobbler.)

[Scene 6: Narad Muni Returns to the Cobbler]

(The scene shifts back to the village where the cobbler is still working. Narad Muni returns to the cobbler, who greets him warmly.)

Cobbler:
Pranam, Narad Muni. Kya aap mere prashna ka uttar lekar aaye hain?

Narad Muni:
Haan, mochi. Bhagwan Vishnu ne kaha hai ki tumhara jeevan jaldi hi samapt hone wala hai. Lekin tum chinta mat karo, sab kuch unki ichcha se hota hai. Aur, tumhari bhakti ke prashn ke uttar mein, Bhagwan Vishnu ne kaha hai ki is samay vah ek haathi ko soyi ke chhed se nikaal rahe hain.

Cobbler:
(laughs with joy)
Kitna adbhut hai! Mera Vishwas aur mazboot ho gaya hai. Bhagwan Vishnu sach mein sab kuch kar sakte hain!

Narad Muni:
(surprised)
Tumhe is baat par vishwas kaise hua? Kya tumhe nahi lagta ki ek haathi ko soyi ke chhed se nikaalna asambhav hai?

Cobbler:
Narad Muni, agar Bhagwan Vishnu is vishva ka palan kar sakte hain, jo itna bada hai, toh unke liye ek haathi ko soyi ke chhed se nikaalna kitna chhota kaam hoga? Mere prabhu kuch bhi kar sakte hain.

BOY1 (Narrator):
Narad Muni ko tab samajh aaya ki ye mochi ek saccha bhakt hai, jo apne prabhu mein poora vishwas rakhta hai. Lekin Brahmin ka ahankar uski bhakti mein badha ban gaya tha. Yehi sachchi bhakti hai—poora vishwas aur nishtha Bhagwan mein.

[Scene 7: Conclusion]

(Narad Muni stands with folded hands before the cobbler, acknowledging his deep devotion.)

Narad Muni:
Tumhari bhakti sach mein prashansaniya hai. Tumne mujhe bhakti ka asli arth samjhaya hai. Tumhara jeevan safal hoga aur tum apne prabhu ke charanon mein sthaan paoge.

(Narad Muni blesses the cobbler and exits, leaving the cobbler smiling peacefully as he continues his work.)

BOY1 (Narrator):
Is prakar mochi ki sacchi bhakti aur Vishnu mein uska poora vishwas Narad Muni ke liye bhi prerna ka kaaran bana. Ye kahani humein yeh sikhati hai ki bhakti sirf karmon se nahi, balki poore vishwas aur nishtha se hoti hai.
